PAC-MAN 3D — HOW TO PLAY

THE GOAL

Eat every dot on all three floors of the castle to clear the level. Four wraiths hunt you. Touch one and you lose a life — lose all three and it's game over. Levels are endless and each one is harder.

THE CASTLE

Three stacked copies of the classic maze: Floor 1 (teal, bottom), Floor 2 (blue, middle — you start here, and the wraiths' keep is here), Floor 3 (violet, top). Your floor renders bright; other floors fade out. The glowing cyan columns are shafts — the only places you can ride up or down. They pulse when you're standing on one.

TUNNELS

The corridor across the middle of every floor exits the maze on both sides and wraps around to the opposite edge, exactly like the arcade. Wraiths re-decide their route at every cell, so a well-timed wrap breaks a chase.

CONTROLS

←↑→↓ / WASD move (relative to the camera) · PGUP / E / SPACE up a floor · PGDN / Q / SHIFT down a floor
C camera view (fixed / follow / top-down) · R recenter view · P pause · DRAG orbit · WHEEL zoom · I this panel · ENTER start

You keep moving in your last direction until you turn or hit a wall. Floor changes are queued: press up/down any time and you'll ride the next shaft you cross (one press = one floor; expires after ~6 s; a ▲ QUEUED hint shows on screen while one is pending).

THE FOUR WRAITHS

Each has a beacon pillar you can see through walls, and its own hunting brain:

BLINKY (crimson, horns) — targets you directly. Fastest to punish hesitation. When few dots remain he speeds up (Cruise Elroy).
PINKY (rose, ribbons) — aims 4 cells ahead of where you're heading — including straight up or down a shaft. Turn often to fake her out.
INKY (cyan, mask) — takes the point 2 cells ahead of you, draws a line from Blinky to it, and doubles it. Unpredictable; watch him near Blinky.
CLYDE (amber, hulking) — chases like Blinky when far, but retreats to his corner when within 8 cells. Use his cowardice to herd him.
WISP (green, flickering flame) — pure chaos. No brain, no pattern: every intersection is a coin flip across all six directions, on every floor. He never hunts you and drifts a little slower — if he kills you, you walked into him. He's also the fifth link in the combo chain.

Wraiths alternate between chase and short scatter breaks (each patrols a corner on a different floor). Every mode switch makes all of them reverse direction — that's your cue. They never reverse otherwise.

POWER PELLETS

The four large pulsing pellets per floor turn the wraiths blue and slow for a few seconds (timer bar at the top; it blinks near the end). Eat them for 200 → 400 → 800 → 1600 → 3200 points per chain — a full five-wraith chain means hunting across floors before the timer dies. Eaten wraiths race home as eyes and are reborn. Frightened time shrinks every level.

FRUITS

At 25% / 50% / 75% of dots eaten, a bonus fruit appears at the classic arcade spot — the center court just below the keep — but on a random floor (gold beacon + chip at the top of the screen with a countdown) for 12 seconds. Values climb with level: Cherry 100, Strawberry 300, Orange 500, Apple 700, Melon 1000, Galaxian 2000, Bell 3000, Key 5000. Detour or not — that's the gamble.

SPEED & LEVELS

Speeds match the original arcade: level 1 you run at 80% of full speed, wraiths at 75%. Levels 2–4: 90% / 85%. Level 5+: 100% / 95%. Scatter breaks shrink and the keep releases wraiths faster every level. Dots: 10 · power pellet: 50.

READING THE HUD

The FLOOR MAP (right) is a live map of the floor you're on: your gold dot, any wraiths currently on your floor (blue while frightened, pale as eyes), cyan shaft squares, and fruit. Below it, the FLOORS panel shows which floor you and every wraith are on, plus the dots left per floor (✓ = cleared) — plan which floor to close out first, and remember Blinky rages when the level's dots run low.
